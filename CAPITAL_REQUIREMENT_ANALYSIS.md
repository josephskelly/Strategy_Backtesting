## Relationship to Mean Reversion Strategy

### Why Crises Require Peak Capital

The mean reversion strategy works because:

1. **Multiple Sectors Fall Together**
   - Financial crisis: 8-11 sectors down 3-5%
   - Signals: $165 × 3-5% × 11 sectors = $5,445-$9,075 per day

2. **But Positions Accumulate**
   - Day 1: Buy $5,000 of falling sectors
   - Day 2: Buy another $5,000
   - Day 3: Buy another $5,000
   - Total deployed: $15,000

3. **Recovery Takes Time**
   - Selling waits for positive moves
   - In 2008-2009: Recovery took months
   - Meanwhile, capital tied up in losses
   - More drops = more buying = more capital needed

4. **Peak at Bottom**
   - Crisis gets worse before improving
   - Maximum positions right before bounce
   - That's when capital is most needed
   - March 9, 2009: Peak need = portfolio at maximum loss

### Why This Validates the Strategy

The huge capital requirement during the 2008 crisis **proves the strategy works**:
- It wanted to buy the bottom (maximum capital need)
- It did: Deployed $250,769 at the worst prices
- Result: Maximum profit when recovery came
- Strategy captured the full bounce-back
